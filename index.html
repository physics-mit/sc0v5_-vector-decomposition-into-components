Explanation of Vector Components

  • Vectors can be represented through their components.
  • A vector A can be decomposed into its components in the x and y-directions (and z-direction for 3D).
  • This is equivalent to finding the projections of vector A along the x, y (and z) axes.
  • 2D Vector: A = Ax * i_hat + Ay * j_hat (where i_hat and j_hat are unit vectors along x and y).
  • 3D Vector: A = Ax * i_hat + Ay * j_hat + Az * k_hat (where k_hat is the unit vector along z).
  • Vectors can be drawn anywhere; they don't have to start at the origin (though this simulator draws them from the origin for clarity).
